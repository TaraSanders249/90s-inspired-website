Titanic

Titanic is an iconic movie that came out in 1997. The movie, featuring Leo DiCaprio as Jack Dawson (spoiler, in case you didn't see the background, he dies) and Kate Winslet as Rose DeWitt Bukater. Titanic, is of course, a love story. However, while the sinking of the ship may have been romanticized, they did a good job at also highlighting the tragedy that was the titanic sinking. When the titanic sank in April of 1912, more than 1500 people died, whereas only 700 survived. There had been enough lifeboats on the titanic for just over half of the ship's population to survive, and yet less than a third lived. The movie didn't exactly highlight the heavy death toll, but to first time viewers of the movie they certainly made sure that the tragedy of the movie was known through the death of one of the main characters--Jack. Jack had only barely been able to make it onto the Titanic, having won the tickets off of someone while gambling. He then spent the rest of the movie charming Rose, who at the time was engaged. In the last half hour of the movie, Rose explained that Jack had died which the audience saw via flashback, and she had secretly taken his last name as her own to avoid her expectations amongst the wealthy. The movie finishes with an elderly Rose throwing her necklace into the sea. Celine Dion's song "My Heart Will Go On" is well known for being part of the Titanic sound track.
